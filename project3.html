The Movie Database (TMBD) Dashboard (1916 - 2016)

Dashboard Overview

It's hard to read each graph on this dashboard, so let's zoom into each one individually.


Director Pie Chart

Selecting only movies with Chinese as the original language, we can see this pie chart's intended use. Here, we see most Chinese movies in TMDB's dataset are pretty equally distributed in amount of movies they've released, with the exception of Zhang Yimou, who has 3 movies in total.


Histogram of Runtime, with Revenue as Color Gradient

This histogram shows all movies in the dataset and their runtimes in bins. Interestingly, you can see that the average revenue per movie increases as the runtime increases.


Frequency by Release Year, with Popularity as Colour Gradient

This time series shows us that newer movies tend to be more popular than old movies. It's important to remember these reviews are likely mostly all recent reviews, as this website/database didn't exist back when most of these older movies were made.


Average Revenue vs. Budget by Director, with Popularity as Bubble Size

This scatter plot shows up how 'efficient' a movie was in its Revenue/Budget ratio. A movie in the top-left of the chart means it had a very high budget and very low revenue, top-right means it had both a very high budget and revenue, bottom-left means it had both very low budget and revenue, and bottom-right shows a very low budget but very high revenue. This is arguable where most directors would want their movies to end up, since this is most financially efficient.
