Sales Distribution Across States

The states with the highest sales were Texas with $170,188, New York with $310,876, and California with $457,688.

The states with the lowest sales were Maine with $1,271, West Virginia with $1,210, and North Dakota with $920.

The most likely reason why Texas, New York, and California have the most sales is probably associated to their population. California is #1 in population, Texas is #2, and New York is #3.

Similarly, Maine is #43 in population, West Virginia is #40, and North Dakota is #48.

This shows us a potential reason to explain the pattern in sales.



Category Sales Comparison

Technology is the category with the highest sales, followed by Furniture, then Office Supplies.

Furniture and Office Supplies follow each other closely, but Technology beats them both by a large margin.



Monthly Sales Trend

Across all products, there does seem to be a few peaks, and a clear seasonality.

The peaks are in March, September, November, and December. The month with the highest sales year for year is November, and the lowest is in February. Sales in general increase rapidly in the last quarter of the year. A simple explanation for this would be that people are buying gifts for the holidays, with a peak in November, we can assume people are getting their early Christmas shopping done. The peak in March is more difficult to explain, except for perhaps people are getting their Spring Cleaning done, and in doing so, need to buy new materials after they've thrown out old things.



Profit vs. Sales Analysis

High Sales, Low Profits:

Tables seem to be the only real high sales low profit sub-category. Low profit is generous considering it seems money is lost on selling tables. Their bulkiness and added difficulty transporting may not be worth the profit gained from selling them.

Low Sales, High Profits:

Copiers by far are the subcategory with the lowest sales and highest profits, following by accessories then paper with very sales. While accessories could be many different things, copier machines and paper are interestingly related.



Sales by Shipping Mode

Most sales by a huge margin are shipping Standard Class, then Second Class, then First Class, and lastly Same Day.

Same Day accounts for the lowest amount of shipping methods, First & Second classes are close together, and Standard dominates the shipping methods by a huge amount. This makes it obvious that even though Same Day may be more expensive, not many people opt for it. Seeing most orders ship in Standard Class could encourage a business to make sure the Standard Class' pricing is correct for how much it costs them to ship that way, as this is the most common shipping method.
